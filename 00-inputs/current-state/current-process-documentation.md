# Current-State Process Documentation

**Prepared by:** Ms. K. Joshi (Data Entry Operator, District Hospital), reviewed by Sr. Nurse M. Pawar
**Date:** July 2026
**Purpose:** Describe how vaccination and OPD intake works today, for the vendor's discovery phase.

---

## A. A citizen's day today (vaccination drive)

1. **Announcement.** A drive (e.g., typhoid, COVID booster) is announced via ASHA workers, posters at the facility, and sometimes the local newspaper. The announcement gives dates, not times.
2. **Arrival.** Citizens begin queueing from ~6:30 AM for a 9:00 AM start. There is no way to know remaining capacity before travelling.
3. **Token.** At the gate, a staff member hands out numbered paper tokens from a tear-off pad. Tokens are strictly first-come. When the pad estimate for the day is exhausted, remaining citizens are told to return another day.
4. **Registration desk.** Name, age, phone number, and (for follow-up doses) the previous dose date are entered by hand into the **facility register** (a bound A3 ledger). If the citizen brought their previous slip, the register entry cross-references it; if not, staff take their word.
5. **Waiting.** Citizens wait in the corridor. Average wait on drive days: 1.5–3 hours. There is no seating for more than ~30 people.
6. **Administration.** Nurse administers the dose, notes the **vaccine name and batch number by hand** in the register and on the citizen's slip.
7. **Slip.** The citizen receives a handwritten slip with a facility stamp: name, vaccine, date, batch, and "next dose due" written in the corner if applicable. This slip IS the certificate. Lost slips require a manual register search to reissue.

## B. OPD (general outpatient)

Same token pattern without the drive-day surge, but daily. Registration and the doctor's notes both go into paper registers. No appointment concept exists at any facility.

## C. Vials and stock, as handled today

- Stock arrives from the district cold-chain store. The facility maintains a **stock register**: vials received, vials opened, doses given, balance.
- A 10-dose vial, once opened, must be used within the manufacturer's window (varies by vaccine; commonly the same session). At day end the nurse-in-charge counts doses given against vials opened; discarded doses are recorded as wastage.
- The open-a-new-vial decision late in the day is made on gut feel about how many more people might turn up.

## D. Follow-up doses, as handled today

- Ms. Joshi transcribes the register weekly into an Excel workbook (one sheet per drive) at the district hospital.
- A "due list" is generated manually each month by filtering the previous drive's sheet.
- The due list is printed and given to ASHA workers, who phone or visit citizens they can reach. There is no confirmation loop; whether a citizen was actually contacted is not tracked.

## E. Reporting, as handled today

- Each facility posts its monthly figures (total administrations by vaccine, wastage, OPD count) to the DHS on a paper form; Ms. Joshi consolidates into Excel.
- The consolidated workbook reaches the DHO typically 3–5 weeks after month end.
- No-show data does not exist, because bookings do not exist.
